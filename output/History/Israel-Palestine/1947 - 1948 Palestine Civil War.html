The initial phase of the 1948 Palestine war and this broke out after the adoption of the Partition Plan mentioned in The United Nations Partition Plan for Palestine, or Resolution (II).

Background

During the period of the British Mandate Palestine had an uphill battle with the Zionist forces and the Palestinian nationalists where there was a period of the Arab Revolt and the opposition the a immigration.

After World War II and [The Holocaust] the Zionist movement gained more attention in recognition of a Jewish state as Zionist groups fought against the British administration. - Examples of Zionist attacks can be seen through the Haganah

Beginning

A response from The United Nations Partition Plan for Palestine, or Resolution (II) from the Arab Higher Committee enacted a general strike lasting of three days and a wind of violence took hold on the country on both sides. - Kfar Sirkin killings - Jewish bus killing - Hadera killings - Jewish bus killing - Shubaki family assassination - Irgun throwing bombs at mass crows of Arabs - Retaliation of 39 jewish people killed

The situation caused the United States to withdraw their support for the Partition Plan and propose a trusteeship for Palestine supporting the Arab League to beleive that the palestinians could put an end to the Partition Plan. Harry Truman was president during this time

Fight and Military Support

  • Zionist's had support of sympathizers in the United States and Stalin's support and recovering stockpiles in World War II.
  • The Jewish Agency set aside foreign arms purchase at 28 million while the Arab Higher Committee total budge was 2,250,000.

Foreign Intervention

Violence intensified and the British did not seek out to take control of the situation in the start. There was a decent amount of intervention with the regions of Syria and Jordan and some of the main cities.

Morale of the fighters

Arab Higher Committee was cofindent and in announcement to the Secretary General:

[!attention] Announcement made to the Secretary-General on 6 February The Palestinian Arabs consider any attempt by Jewish people or by whatever power or group of power to establish a Jewish state in an Arab territory to be an act of aggression that will be resisted by force [...]
The prestige of the United Nations would be better served by abandoning this plan and by not imposing such an injustice [...]
The Palestinian Arabs make a grave declaration before the UN, before God and before history that they will never submit to any power that comes to Palestine to impose a partition. The only way to establish a partition is to get rid of them all: men, women, and children.

The morale of Jewish leaders was not high distress and despair arose clearly from the notes taken at the meetings of the Mapai party and the attacks against Jewish settlements and main roads worsened. Although, the Haganah remained superior in number and quality to the Arab forces.

First Wave of Palestinian Refugees

The morale of the Arab fighters was not shared among the civilians and Arabs in the mostly upper to middle class range fled in large numbers in steady exodus.

Foreign Policies

  • Britain did not want a Palestine state led by the mufti and discussed facilitating the entry of the Arab legion.
  • United States had a turnabout with a proposal to convoke a special assembly on the issue
  • Stalin breached embargo and supported the Yishuv from Czechoslovakia
  • Arab leaders avoided being directly involved in then support for the Palestinians
    • at the end of the cairo summit the arab leaders formed the Arab Liberation Army that united all the heads of major states

Power Balance

  • Arab Liberation Army was financed by Arab League - in theory - funding never came
  • The palestinian forced lacked a lot of arms with limited foreign support and some fighters had to be content with what was on the black market.
  • The Jewish fighers also had a lack of arms in the start with 1/3 -> 2/3 fighters having arms but the Haganah sent agents to Europe and the United States in order to arm and equip the army

Reorganisation of Haganah

  • Ben-Gurion shaped the Haganah from being a paramilitary to an army.

War of the roads and blockade of Jerusalem

  • settlements were dispered Battle of Jerusalem
  • Jerusalem was difficult to access and became more critical to the jewish population
  • Abd al-Qadir's zone of influence, Operation Atlas coordinating with al-Husayni to plan a disruption to blockcade western jerusalem

Haganah Offensive - Second Phase

  • Arab forced developed into 10,000 men however the palestinian groups still remained defeated and the yishuv took control of the principal routes that linked the jewish settlements additionally Jerusalem was able to keep its supply, palestinian society collapsed, and many mixed cities were taken by Haganah.
  • Plan Dalet is a historically controversial plan that was the rules and objectives of the Haganah
    • was seen to some people as a plan of conquering palestine or ethnically cleansing the region
  • Operation Nachson
    • plan to open up supplies to jerusalem
    • arab villages that blocked the route were either destroyed or taken
  • Battle of Mishmar HaEmek
    • arab attack against yishuv
  • Deir Yassin massacre
    • irgun attacking village
  • Hadassah medical convoy massacre
    • revenge for Deir Yassin massacre
  • Battle of Ramat Yohanan and the defection of the Druze
  • Siege of mixed localities
    • urban centers attacked by the Jewish forces, Arabs inhabitants of the towns were expelled en masse
  • Operation Yiftah
  • Meeting of Golda Meir and King Abdullah I of Jordan (10 May)
    • Discussion of the situation with Abdullah
  • Kfar Etzion massacre
    • arab attacks of Israeli Settlements
  • Jerusalem: Operations Yevusi and Kilshon ("Pitchfork") (13–18 May)
  • Operation Ben-Ami (13–22 May)
  • Main wave of the Palestinian Arab exodus
  • Preparations made by the Arab League

Results

  • decisive Jewish victory
  • The Yishuv proved it had the ability to defend itself and the Palestinian military was crushed
  • Jewish forces captured four cities and 190 villages and the refugees flood Samaria and Galilee
  • only a few young Arab men participated in the fighting against Jerusalem
  • David Ben-Gurion declares the establishment of the State of Israel and the 1948 Palestine war is started with the start of the Arab-Israeli War
