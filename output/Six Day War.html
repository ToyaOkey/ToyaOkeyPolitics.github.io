Also know as the third Arab-Israeli War was fought between Israel and a coalition of Arab states, mainly Egypt, Syria, and Jordan from June 5 - June 10th 1967.

Military hoistilities broke out amid poor relationships between israel and Arab neighbors in observing the 1949 Armistice Agreements signed during the first Arab-Israeli War. In 1945 there were regional tenstions of the Straight of Tiran and what became known as the Suez Crisis.

The tensions arised over the egyption president Gamal Abdel Nassar annoucing the Straits of Tiran would be closed to Israeli vessels and mobilized the Egyptian military into defensife lines along the border of israel and ordered the withdrawal of all UNEF personnel.

June 5, 1967, then UNEF was in process of leaving the zone and Israel launched a series of strikes against Egyptian forces. Egypt was caught by suprise and nearly all aeriel military assess were destroyed giving Israel air offensive into the Sinai Peninsula and the Gaza Strip which was occupied by Egypt during this time.

After some resistence, Nassar ordered an evacuation of the Sinai Peninsula by the sixth day of the conflict and Israel occupied the Sinai Peninsula. Jordan entered into a defense pact with Egypt a week before the war began and launched attacks against the Israel Defense Forces - IDF to slow israel's advance. On the fifth day Syria joined the war by shelling positions in the north.

Egypt and Jordan agreed to a ceasefire on June 8th and Syria on June 9th and signed with Israel on June 11th. The war resulted in several Arab fatalities and signiificantly less israeli fatalities. - USS Liberty incident

At the time israel had occupied the Golan Heights from Syria West Bank and East Jerusalem from Jordan and Gaza Strip from Egypt. Several Arab's were displaced as a result of the war and the Suez Canal was closed until 1975.
