Preference Assumptions

  1. Completeness - the assumption that consumers can compare and rank all possible combinations of goods and services
  2. Transitivity - if you prefer A to B and B to C, you prefer B to C
  3. Non Satiation - more if better than less

Indifference Curves - graphical maps of preferences - Four properties - consumers prefer higher indifference curves - Indifference curves are downward sloping - violates non satiation if not met because the points would be on the same curve - Indifference curves never* cross - crossing violates transitivity - There is only one indifference curve through every bundle -
