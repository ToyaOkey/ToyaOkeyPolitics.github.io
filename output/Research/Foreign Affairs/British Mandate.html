geographical entity that existed under the region of Palestine also referred to as Mandatory Palestine between 1920 - 1948 1

Background Information

Emerged from the Ottoman Empire collapsing after World War I allowing the british and French to gain control over the region

Led the British Government to recognize the independence of different countries after the war in exchange for Husayn ibn Aly which led to the Arab Revolt against the Ottoman Empire and the Sykes-Picot Agreement divided the Middle East into several different British and French spheres of influence and the Balfour Declaration which the British Government committed itself to a national home for the Jewish people

Seems that the idea was Britain was given a "dual mandate" system on behalf of the current inhabitants of Palestine while also giving Jewish people a settlement for their haven.

Mandate Period

Over the mandate, two systems were developed under the framework, one under Arab control and the other under Jewish control. Each society has it's own Socioeconomic framework and became isolated from each other

More tensions started to arise as the groups became isolated from another

Zionism movements operated under the axes of land acquisition and immigration which including buying land from Arab's. Palestine population grew from 700K - 1.8 million while the Jewish population grew under more General Immigration tenfold

Jewish-Arab Conflict

During the 1920s - 1930s, violent actions between Jewish people and Arabs took place, known as the Wailing Wall Riots, which is considered a turning point.

Arabs did not distinguish between the Jewish people of Arab origin and the Jewish people of eastern European origin which viewed that all as a homogenous group with all the same interests, led to a conclusion that a Jewish state is needed.

Response from British: Shaw Commission which sought out a review of Jewish immigration and land purchasing.

More conflicts aroused and led to an Arab Revolt which was triggered by and economic crisis and mass immigration of Jewish people which increased after Adolf Hitler took power in Germany during World War II. Royal Commission was established which concluded that Palestine had two different societies making it necessarily to Partition Plan. Arab Higher Committee rejected the recommendations and led to another revolt. Britain's response was marked by violent force and the Arab Higher Committee was dissolved and declared and illegal body.

In response to the revolt, the British Government issue the White Paper which weakened the Arab society and after World War II failed to recover until 1948 Palestine war

The negative opinions of the situation within Palestine made the mandate unpopular within Britain and led the termination and involvement of the united nations.

After the UN General Assembly, it was announced the mandate ended effective May 15 1948 and on May 14 1948 at midnight the state of Israel declared independence
